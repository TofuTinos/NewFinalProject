Will Terry - Children's Book Illustrator
Will Terry has been a freelance illustrator for 30 years. After finishing his BFA project at BYU he began working for magazines and newspapers not far from where he grew up in Washington D.C. His early clients include publications such as Time, Money, Wall Street Journal and ads for Sprint, Pizza hut, M&M Mars, Fed Ex, and Master Card. He has illustrated about 30 children's books for Random House, Simon Schuster, Scholastic, Penguin, Klutz, and Albert Whitman.
Trent Kaniuga - Game Designer and Illustrator
Trent Kaniuga is a concept artist who has worked on major title video games such as Fortnite, World of Warcraft, Diablo 3, League of Legends, Overwatch, Hearthstone, and many others. He also the creator of Twilight Monk one of his own major projects, Ikeda and CreeD. One the side he has a YouTube channel dedicated to art tutorials and career advice.
Brad Colbow - Illustrator and Youtuber
Brad Colbow is a professional graphic designer and Illustrator. He has published a variety of comics, but he is known for having a youtube channel dedicated to educating people on graphic design and helping beginners with some fundamentals of art. On the same channel he does several reviews on what kind of tech is useful for digital art such as ipads, tablets, and other digital art tools.
Jake Parker - Illustrator
Jake Parker is an American comics short-story creator, concept artist, illustrator, and animator. He had developed this interest at a young age, after seeing the works of cartoonists such as Bill Watterson and Jim Lee. Now however Jake Parker is well known for working with Blue Skys studio on projects such as Horton Hears A Who and Rio.
Riki Fuhrmann
Riki Fuhrmann is an art designer and director who works with HAL Laboratory, A major video game company that is responsible for bringing Kirby, one of the biggest game titles to fruition. After many years of working with the company he was promoted to the director role on a major tile "Kirby: Planent Robobot." With this he became a main staple on the art design for the kirby titles for the next seven years.
